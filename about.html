So, what's all this about?

Ever wanted to add a few cool interactive aspects to your builds but tutorials don't quite catch your attention? Are wiki pages with more information than you need at the moment not really your cup of tea?

So many redstone resources out there are wonderfully informational, yet not everything is digestible enough for those who don't yet know much about redstone.

The goal of this site is to provide a resource for those who only want quick, bite-sized bits of information about redstone components and mechanics, either as an introduction to the field as a whole, or to learn just enough to build a simple circuit to complete a build.

Who's behind this site?

I'm a full-time college student who enjoys redstone engineering in my free time. I'm nowhere near an expert, but I do like to think I understand a broad swathe of what there is to know about redstone. I built this site in the hopes of sharing the enjoyment of designing and building circuits with all others who wish to learn even the slightest bit. I began self-learning HTML and CSS in summer of 2026, so please bear with any faulty features!
